CD Projekt Red - is this my next Mitsubishi Heavy?

December 26, 2024

The entire thought process started in mid 2023 when I was looking at 10-Q of mag 7 (Apple, Amazon and Microsoft) during the dotcom bubble. Excluding some awful years due to the macro environments, good companies have the ability to have good free cash flow. They re-invest their money, expand their business, and make more revenue and profit next year. At least to me, a good business is just that simple. Ideally, it has little competition, and stays unnoticed. And if they can expand freely, I think buying back stocks can be a really dumb idea. However, Nvidia and CD projekt both bought back some stocks this year.

The gaming industry is truly fascinating. Several key elements contribute to a successful game. First, the gameplay must be engaging and enjoyable. A compelling story is also essential. Furthermore, developers must excel at marketing their games to generate strong revenues.

Although CD Projekt Red now focuses solely on RPGs - following several unsuccessful ventures into mobile gaming—it has achieved tremendous success and generated substantial profits through its RPG titles. It boasts one of the best marketing teams in the industry, having brought its flagship franchises, Cyberpunk and The Witcher, to the screen through collaborations with Netflix. It is one of the only two companies in the industry that maintain high profit margin. - Only one, I should say. Another just got acquired by Microsoft for 70 billion. And I am not here to judge whether they achieved this by underpaying their workers.

I came across an intriguing post about CD Projekt. While it likely reflects common perceptions about the company, I found its arguments idiotic and mathematically flawed in almost every respect. For 7 of his investing ideas, he seemed to be wrong on 6 of them. Man, that's way worse than coin toss. The only win is a mere 20% since 2022 - Arguably that is worse than buying short term bonds. This is the internet, so let’s indulge in some well-deserved shaming. Jeez, look at his other postings! Behold this poor fool and his utterly pathetic, shitty, stinky commodity picks. Honestly, it’s a miracle he hasn’t committed seppuku out of sheer embarrassment.

Before we even get to the numbers, let’s take a moment to admire just how laughably stupid and utterly devoid of research this so-called “short report” is - better described as a formal invitation to bankruptcy:

AAA single player games have short life cycles.

Then this genius decides to compare CD Projekt with Take-Two because, apparently, completely different business models and earnings profiles don’t matter. Bro, have you even glanced at their earnings history? Spot any differences? By the way, it is way harder to value a business like Take-Two but this dude talks like a pro. What makes things funnier is that top game producers aren't even competing with each other but this dude obviously believes so. From a Ubisoft earnings call the CEO said that the release of GTA6 will boost their sales of that year and that's how things work.

CDR is a “one game at a time” studio, averaging 4-5 years between releases.

This is bullshit. What is it with company's valuation? You calculate value based on future cash flow, not some stupid narratives or publishing frequencies. And numbers will adjust themselves. But let me tell you a secret: CD Projekt is no longer a “one game at a time” studio. It kept 30% profit margin. It kept shipping its crappy products. It fixed all the problems, and moved on to the next project. Built two new studios in North America. Now CD Projekt is starting to develop three projects at the same time.

Now, let's talk about the numbers and why CD Projekt Red is attractive. First I want to talk about a major point, base number of players.

My guess is that the number of gamers have increased dramatically in the past decade.

CD Projekt Red's revenues for the period 2020–2023 are as follows (in billion PLN): 2.14, 0.89, 0.95, and 1.23. If the base number of players hadn't increased during this period, it would have been unlikely for them to achieve 1.23 billion in 2023 (even with the release of DLC, phantom liberty). An alternative explanation is that the revenue in 2020 was significantly reduced due to Cyberpunk refund requests. In that case, the estimated revenue for 2020 could have been around 3 billion.

Now, let's compare this with The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, which generated 0.80 billion PLN in revenue in 2015. The player base roughly tripled between 2015 and 2020 (if not accounting for price increase), and it has continued to grow following the pandemic. This raises the question—what will the numbers look like in 2027 when The Witcher 4 is released?

Another evidence is the views of game trailer on Youtube. Red Dead Redemption 2, arguably a much more successful game, achieved 15 million views since 2018. Witcher 4 achieved half of that number in two weeks. I don't want to discuss about the physics, entropy, or whatever behind this. They achieved 8 million views in 2 weeks and that is enough. Only shitheads think they are capable of explaining everything. I just need to know the boundary of not making big mistakes.

Second point is that they are expanding because they have good profits.

This is the feature that I love the most about good growth stocks. And CD Projekt is doing that. The expansion is veiled by its cyclicality. And when it shows, I get rewarded from stock prices. As said earlier, CD Projekt is now working on three franchises at the same time. The Witcher 4-6, Cyberpunk sequel, and a new title. The latter two are still in concept phase but soon they will catch up.

For Witcher 4-6, they will probably be released in 2027, 2030 and 2033. The Witcher 5-6 will have quicker production time because the larger framework is complete during the development of Witcher 4. The Cyberpunk sequel will come out in, say, 2029 and the new title comes out in 2035.

I don't want to show my calculation of future earnings, but based on the assumptions above, you will probably get an annual revenue that is close to its current market cap post 2033. And cyclicality will be greatly reduced for this company. Assuming 30-45% profit margin, that's quite exciting. The ultimate shape of CD Projekt Red should look like Capcom: several addictive RPG game franchises, constant cooperation with Hollywood... Capcom currently trades at 42 P/E ratio and apply that to a 2033 earnings projection of CD Projekt Red and you get a 14 (42*0.35) bagger from here. And do you need to bother wether to use net earnings or EBIT or EBITDA? Does that even matter? You are probably buying a growth company at a lower valuation than that of Mitsubishi Heavy Industries at its most broken time. You know what I learned from Mitsubishi? Redemption takes time because people are very stubborn and stupid. And when the public perception changes, it overshoots.
